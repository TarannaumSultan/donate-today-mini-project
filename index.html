Food for a family

Food for a family in flood relief typically includes essential items such as rice, lentils, cooking oil, canned goods, clean drinking water, and non-perishable food items. These provisions are vital to sustain a family's nutritional needs during a flood crisis, ensuring they have access to safe and nourishing meals amidst the challenging circumstances. Additionally, food aid may also include items like hygiene kits and baby formula for families with infants to address their specific needs.

Shelter for a family

Providing shelter for a family affected by flooding is crucial to ensure their safety and well-being. Temporary shelters, such as sturdy tents or emergency housing, offer protection from the elements and a safe space for families to regroup and rebuild their lives. These shelters typically include essential supplies like bedding, clean water, and sanitation facilities, offering comfort and security during a challenging time. By offering shelter, we help families regain a sense of stability and hope as they work towards recovery after a devastating flood.

Education for a child

Education for a child in flood relief involves providing access to safe and stable learning environments, ensuring continuity of education during and after floods, and offering psychosocial support to help children cope with trauma. It's vital to prioritize education to empower children with the knowledge and skills they need to build a better future despite the challenges posed by flooding and its aftermath.
